Intel Project - Data Analysis for Sustainability

This project involves analyzing energy production and consumption data across different U.S. regions to identify the optimal location for Intel’s new data center, focusing on areas with energy surpluses and high renewable energy generation.

The Intel Project - Data Analysis for Sustainability focuses on helping Intel identify the optimal location for a new data center by analyzing regional energy production, consumption, and the use of renewable resources. The project leverages SQL to query large datasets that provide insights into energy generation, demand, and power plant operations across the United States. By examining which regions produce surplus energy and rely heavily on renewable sources, Intel aims to prioritize sustainable and cost-effective locations for their expansion.

Throughout the project, SQL queries are used to aggregate data from multiple tables, including energy production by plant and regional energy demand. Visualizations created in Tableau further illustrate trends in renewable versus fossil fuel energy generation, highlighting regions with the highest renewable energy capacity and potential for growth. This comprehensive analysis allows Intel’s Sustainability Team to make data-driven decisions that align with the company’s environmental goals and operational efficiency.
